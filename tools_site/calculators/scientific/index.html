Description

The Scientific Calculator is a powerful computational tool designed for advanced mathematics, engineering, and scientific tasks. Unlike standard calculators, this tool allows you to perform complex operations such as trigonometry, logarithms, and exponentiation with ease. We've built this interface to mimic the layout of professional handheld scientific calculators, ensuring familiarity for students and professionals alike. Whether you are solving calculus problems, analyzing physics equations, or working on statistical data, this tool provides the precision you need. It runs entirely in your browser, offering a lag-free experience without creating any server-side logs of your work.

What It Does

It handles advanced mathematical functions including Sine, Cosine, Tangent (and their inverses), Logarithms (log, ln), Square Roots, Powers, and Factorials. It also supports constant values like Pi (π) and Euler's number (e).

How To Use

  • Basic Math: Use the standard number pad for arithmetic.
  • Trigonometry: Click 'sin', 'cos', or 'tan' for trigonometric functions. Output is generally in radians/degrees based on standard JS math libraries (defaulting to radians).
  • Advanced Functions: Use 'sqrt' for roots and '^' for powers.
  • Clear: Press 'AC' to reset your calculation workspace.

Who It Is For

This tool is essential for high school and college students, engineers, scientists, and math enthusiasts who need more than just simple addition and subtraction.

Freqently Asked Questions

Is the output in Degrees or Radians?

The calculator uses standard mathematical functions which default to Radians. Please convert accordingly if you need Degree output.

Can I nest functions?

Yes, you can perform operations like sin(45) + log(100), provided you calculate them step-by-step or sequentially.

Is this accurate enough for engineering?

It utilizes 64-bit floating-point precision, which is sufficient for most academic and general engineering tasks, though specialized software is recommended for mission-critical simulations.
