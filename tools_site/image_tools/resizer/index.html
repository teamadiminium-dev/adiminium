Aspect Ratio Lock: By default, the "Lock" icon is active. This ensures that if you change the Width, the Height changes automatically to prevent your image from looking "squashed" or "stretched".

Resizing vs. Cropping

It's important to distinguish:

  • Resizing (This Tool): Changes the resolution of the entire image. A 1000x1000 photo becomes 500x500. The content remains the same, just smaller.
  • Cropping: Cuts away parts of the image content. A 1000x1000 photo becomes 500x500, but now you only see the center face, not the background.

Interpolation Algorithms

When you resize an image, the computer has to guess how to blend pixels (Downscaling) or invent new pixels (Upscaling). We use the browser's native High Quality Bi-Cubic Interpolation to ensure smooth edges and sharp details.

  • 1080 x 1920: TikTok / Instagram Story (Vertical).
  • 1200 x 630: Facebook / Twitter (X) Link Preview card.
  • 32 x 32: Browser Favicon.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make small images bigger (Upscale)?

You can, but "Upscaling" cannot create detail that isn't there. If you turn a 100px icon into a 1000px image, it will look blurry or blocked. For that, you would need AI Upscaling (different tech).

Why is the file size smaller?

Pixel Count = File Size. A 4000x3000 photo has 12 Million pixels. Resizing it to 800x600 reduces it to 480,000 pixels. That's 96% fewer pixels to store!
